# Solar Consultation Closing Script & Call-to-Action

## Closing Framework Overview

### The Three-Step Close
1. **Summary of Value:** Recap the specific benefits for this prospect
2. **Address Final Concerns:** Handle any remaining objections
3. **Clear Call-to-Action:** Specific next steps with urgency

### Psychology of the Close
- **Loss Aversion:** Emphasize what they'll lose by not acting
- **Social Proof:** Reference similar homeowners' success
- **Scarcity:** Limited installation slots or expiring incentives
- **Commitment Consistency:** Small yeses lead to bigger commitments

## Closing Scripts by Prospect Type

### The Financial-Focused Prospect
**Value Summary:**
"So based on your $[monthly bill] electricity bill, you're looking at:
- **$[monthly savings] per month in immediate savings**
- **$[total savings] over 25 years**
- **Complete payback in [X] years**
- **$[home value increase] added to your home value**

The numbers are clear - this is a financial investment that pays you back every single month."

**Closing Question:**
"Would you prefer to lock in these savings now, or would you rather keep paying the utility company more each year?"

### The Environmentally-Conscious Prospect
**Value Summary:**
"With solar, you'll be:
- **Reducing your carbon footprint by [X] tons of CO2**
- **Equivalent to planting [Y] trees**
- **Powering your home with 100% clean energy**
- **Creating a better future for your kids/grandkids**

And you'll save $[amount] while doing it."

**Closing Question:**
"Are you ready to start making a difference while also securing your financial future?"

### The Security-Minded Prospect
**Value Summary:**
"Solar gives you complete energy security:
- **Lock in your energy costs for 25 years**
- **Never worry about utility rate increases again**
- **Power through most outages with battery backup**
- **Energy independence from foreign oil**

You'll have peace of mind knowing your family will always have power."

**Closing Question:**
"How important is it to you to never worry about rising electricity costs again?"

## Specific Closing Techniques

### The Assumptive Close
**Scenario:** Prospect seems ready but hesitant
**Script:** "So should we schedule your installation for the first week of next month, or would the second week work better for you?"

### The Choice Close
**Scenario:** Prospect needs options
**Script:** "We have two great options for you:
1. **$0-down financing** - Start saving immediately
2. **Pay in cash** - Maximum savings with 30% tax credit

Which option fits your financial situation better?"

### The Urgency Close
**Scenario:** Prospect is procrastinating
**Script:** "The 30% federal tax credit is scheduled to decrease next year, and with utility rates going up 4% annually, every month you wait costs you about $[amount] in lost savings."

### The Testimonial Close
**Scenario:** Prospect needs social proof
**Script:** "Your neighbor [Name] just installed their system last month and is already saving $[amount]. They told me they wish they'd done it years ago. Would you like me to share their contact information?"

### The Question Close
**Scenario:** Prospect has unstated concerns
**Script:** "Based on everything we've discussed, what's the one thing holding you back from moving forward today?"

## Handling Final Objections in Close

### "I Need to Talk to My Spouse"
**Response:** "That's absolutely understandable. Let me suggest two options:
1. **I can schedule a joint consultation** with both of you this week
2. **I can prepare a complete information package** for you to review together

Which would work better? And just so you know, our installation calendar is booking up about [X] weeks out."

### "I Need to Think About It"
**Response:** "I completely understand this is a big decision. Let me ask you this - what specific aspect do you need to think about? Is it:
- The financial investment?
- The installation process?
- The equipment specifics?
- Something else I haven't addressed?"

### "I Want to Get Other Quotes"
**Response:** "That's smart - you should absolutely compare options. Let me make this easy for you:
- **Here's our itemized quote** with exact equipment specifications
- **Here's what to look for** when comparing quotes
- **I encourage you to ask competitors** about their warranties and installation quality

Can I follow up with you on [specific day] to answer any questions that come up?"

## Call-to-Action Templates

### Consultation Booking CTA
**Primary Offer:** "I'd like to schedule your free, no-obligation consultation where we'll:
1. **Analyze your roof's solar potential** with satellite imaging
2. **Create your personalized savings projection**
3. **Show you exact financing options** with no money down
4. **Answer all your questions** with no pressure

The consultation takes 30 minutes and can be virtual or in-person. Would Tuesday at 2 PM or Thursday at 4 PM work better for you?"

### Immediate Action CTA
**For Hot Leads:** "Based on your situation, I'm confident we can save you significant money. I have a consultation opening tomorrow at [time] and another on [day] at [time]. Which should I reserve for you?"

### Soft CTA
**For Cautious Prospects:** "Would you like me to send you a customized savings report for your home? No obligation, just detailed information about what solar could do for your specific situation."

## Closing Success Metrics

### Key Performance Indicators
- **Consultation booking rate:** % of qualified prospects who book consultations
- **Show rate:** % of booked consultations that actually happen
- **Conversion rate:** % of consultations that become sales
- **Average time to close:** Days from first contact to signed contract

### Conversion Optimization
- **A/B test** different closing scripts
- **Track** which objections require most follow-up
- **Monitor** seasonal trends in closing rates
- **Analyze** demographic differences in closing approaches

## Closing Scripts for Different Channels

### Phone Closing
**Advantages:** Real-time conversation, immediate objection handling
**Best Practices:**
- Stand up while talking (energy comes through)
- Use verbal nods and acknowledgments
- Mirror their pace and tone
- Ask for the appointment, not the sale

### Email Closing
**Advantages:** Detailed information, can be shared with spouse
**Best Practices:**
- Use clear, scannable formatting
- Include specific numbers and benefits
- Provide clear next steps
- Add social proof and testimonials

### In-Person Closing
**Advantages:** Personal connection, can show materials
**Best Practices:**
- Use body language effectively
- Bring visual aids (savings calculator, testimonials)
- Read their non-verbal cues
- Create a comfortable, no-pressure environment

## Common Closing Mistakes to Avoid

### Don't Say:
- "So, do you want to buy solar?" (Too direct)
- "What's it going to take to get you to sign today?" (Too aggressive)
- "This offer expires tonight" (False urgency)
- "All your neighbors are doing it" (Shaming)

### Instead Say:
- "Based on your savings, which financing option makes the most sense?"
- "What questions do you have before we move forward?"
- "Current incentives are scheduled to change, so the sooner we act, the more you save"
- "Many homeowners in your area are already seeing significant savings"

## Post-Close Follow-up

1. **Send confirmation email** with appointment details

- **Preparation checklist** for what to have ready
- **Brief overview** of what to expect
