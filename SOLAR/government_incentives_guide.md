# Government Incentives & Tax Credits Guide

## Federal Incentives (2024)

### Residential Clean Energy Credit
- **Amount:** 30% of total system cost
- **Eligibility:** Primary residence, second homes qualify
- **No Income Limits:** Available to all homeowners
- **Expiration:** Currently set to decrease to 26% in 2033, 22% in 2034
- **How to Claim:** File IRS Form 5695 with your tax return

### What's Covered:
- Solar panels (PV cells)
- Solar water heaters
- Battery storage (when installed with solar)
- Labor costs and permitting fees
- Sales tax on eligible equipment

## State-Specific Programs

### California
- **CALSSHA:** Up to $3,000 rebate for battery storage
- **SGIP:** Additional incentives for energy storage
- **Property Tax Exemption:** Solar system value excluded from property tax assessment

### New York
- **NY-Sun:** Up to $5,000 rebate for residential solar
- **NYSERDA:** Additional incentives for low-to-moderate income households
- **Property Tax Abatement:** 100% exemption for solar system value

### Texas
- **No State Income Tax:** Federal credit has maximum impact
- **Local Utility Rebates:** Varies by utility company (Austin Energy, CPS Energy, etc.)
- **Net Metering:** Available in most areas

### Florida
- **Property Tax Exemption:** 100% exemption for renewable energy systems
- **Sales Tax Exemption:** No sales tax on solar equipment
- **Net Metering:** Required by state law

### Arizona
- **State Tax Credit:** 25% credit up to $1,000
- **Utility Rebates:** APS, SRP, and Tucson Electric offer various incentives
- **Property Tax Exemption:** Solar system value excluded

### Massachusetts
- **SMART Program:** Performance-based incentives
- **Mass Solar Loan:** Low-interest financing options
- **Property Tax Exemption:** 100% exemption for solar system value

### New Jersey
- **Successor Solar Incentive (SUSSI):** Performance-based payments
- **CEBF Rebates:** Up to $10,000 for battery storage
- **Property Tax Exemption:** Solar system value excluded

## Local Utility Programs

### Net Metering Benefits
- **How it works:** Excess solar energy is sent to the grid for credit
- **Credit rates:** Varies by utility (typically retail rate or close to it)
- **Roll-over:** Unused credits typically roll over month-to-month
- **Annual true-up:** Some utilities settle annually

### Time-of-Use (TOU) Rates
- **Peak savings:** Solar generation during peak hours maximizes savings
- **Battery storage:** Can store energy for use during expensive peak periods
- **Rate optimization:** Solar + battery can significantly reduce TOU bills

## Financing Options

### Solar Loans
- **0% Down Options:** Available through many lenders
- **Interest Rates:** Typically 3-8% APR
- **Terms:** 10-25 year terms available
- **Tax Benefits:** Interest may be tax-deductible

### Solar Leases
- **No Upfront Cost:** Installer owns and maintains system
- **Fixed Monthly Payment:** Predictable energy costs
- **Maintenance Included:** Typically covered by lease provider

### Power Purchase Agreements (PPA)
- **Pay Per Kilowatt:** Fixed rate lower than utility
- **No Ownership:** Installer owns and maintains system
- **Performance Guarantee:** System production guaranteed

## Qualification Requirements

### Basic Eligibility
- **Home Ownership:** Must own the home
- **Credit Score:** 640+ for most financing options
- **Roof Condition:** Suitable for solar installation
- **Utility Service:** Must be connected to the grid

### Income-Based Programs
- **Low-Income Programs:** Additional incentives available
- **Community Solar:** Options for renters or unsuitable roofs
- **HUD Programs:** Special financing for qualified households

## Application Process

### Step 1: Site Assessment
- Roof inspection and measurement
- Energy usage analysis
- System design and proposal

### Step 2: Permitting
- Local building permits
- Utility interconnection agreement
- HOA approval (if applicable)

### Step 3: Installation
- Professional installation (1-3 days typical)
- Utility inspection
- System commissioning

### Step 4: Incentive Processing
- Federal tax credit claimed with tax return
- State rebates applied by installer
- Utility enrollment for net metering

## Important Considerations

### Tax Credit Timing
- **Claim Year:** Credit claimed in year system is placed in service
- **Carryforward:** Excess credit can be carried forward up to 20 years
- **No Refund:** Credit cannot exceed tax liability (except for certain cases)

### System Requirements
- **New Installation:** Must be new, not used
- **Primary Residence:** Must be installed at your primary or secondary home
- **Certification:** Equipment must meet specific certification standards

### Documentation Needed
- **Proof of Purchase:** Receipts and contracts
- **Installation Date:** When system became operational
- **System Specifications:** Size and equipment details
- **Utility Statements:** Before and after installation

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I combine incentives?
Yes! Federal credits can be combined with most state and local incentives.

### What if I sell my home?
- **Financed System:** Can transfer to buyer or pay off at sale
- **Owned System:** Adds to home value, buyer benefits from remaining incentives
- **Leased System:** Typically transferable to new owner

### How long do incentives last?
- **Federal Credit:** Currently 30% through 2032, then phases down
- **State Programs:** Vary by state, some have limited funding
- **Utility Rebates:** Often first-come, first-served

### What if my tax liability is low?
- **Carryforward:** Unused credit can be carried forward up to 20 years
- **Partnership:** Spouses can combine credits if filing jointly
- **Alternative:** Some states offer cash rebates instead of tax credits
